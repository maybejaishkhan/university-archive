## Sources of Information
Divided into 3 categories based on the originality and directness of the reporter.
- **Primary Sources** -> They describe the "actually happened" event without any interpretation or commentary.
- **Secondary Sources** -> They provide commentary on a primary source and add a personal view to the event.
- **Tertiary Sources** -> Compilation (reformatting/condensing) of a secondary source into an easy-readable form.

| Primary            | Secondary       | Tertiary      |
| ------------------ | --------------- | ------------- |
| Autobiographies    | Biographies     | Summaries     |
| Letters            | Articles        | Guidebooks    |
| Literature Works   | History Books   | Chronology    |
| Patents            | Research Papers | Text Books    |
| Interviews/Surveys | Reviews         | Study Results |
| Speeches/Images    | Commentaries    | Compilations  |

## Organization
A group of people working together, organized, to accomplish a goal. It is where individuals cooperate to conduct business under some defined rules.

**Why are organizations required?** -> When a business grows, it becomes necessary to have a disciplined approach to management and results in the making of "organizational structures" which allows for efficient management of business operations.

**For-Profit Organization** -> Their main goal is to maximize shareholder value, often measured by the price of the company stock.
**Non-Profit Organization** -> They do not have "profit" as the main goal and include social/religious groups.

Organizations have many things that make them different from one another.
- **Size** -> How large is the organization?
- **Structure** -> How is the organization structured?
- **Culture** -> What is the workplace culture like?
- **Decision Makers** -> Who is in-charge of making decisions?
- **Interested Parties** -> Internal and External entities that are interested in the organization

## Levels of Management
- Top-Level Management
In any organization top management is the ultimate source of authority. It establishes goals and policies for the enterprise and devotes more time on the planning and co-ordinating functions.
- Middle-Level Management 
They receive orders and instructions from top management and get the things done through lower level management. They are responsible to the top management for the functioning of their departments.
- Lower-Level Management 
Lower-Level managers guide and direct the workers under the instructions from middle level managers.
